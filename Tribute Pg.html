Tribute Page : A. P. J. Abdul Kalam


Avul Pakir Jainlabdeen Abduul Kalam was the 11th president of the country from 2002 to 2007 and is popularely also know as the Missile-Man of India.


Born is a poor family in Tamil Nadu's Rameswaram, Kalam went on to study physics an aerospace engineering and worked with Defence Reasearch and Development Organization(DRDO) and Indian Space Reasearch Organization (ISRO). He also played a role in Pokhran-II tests under the leadership of Atar Bihari Vajpayee.


Abdul Kalam was felicitated with a Padma Bhushan in 1981, a Padma Vibhushan in 1990 and then recieved the highest civilian honour Bharat Ratna in 1997. He is also known for a humble and respectful attitude towards people and was called the People's President.


After the end of his tenure as President of the country, he went back to delivering lectures to students and writing.


He died after suffering a cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at Indian Institute of Management, Shillong, in July 27, 2015. He was buried in his hometown, Rameswaram with full honours.
